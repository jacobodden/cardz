# Game
Up And Down The Rivers

## Description
Up and Down the River is a trick-taking card game where the core challenge is
bidding the exact number of tricks you will win. Taking too many tricks is just
as bad as taking too few.

Here is the comprehensive guide to setup, gameplay, and winning strategies.

## Setup:
- Players: 3 to 7 players (best with 4 to 6).
- The Deck: One standard 52-card deck. Aces are high; 2s are low.
- The "River" Structure: The game progresses through rounds where the hand size changes.
- Going Up: Round 1 starts with 1 card dealt to each player. Round 2 has 2
cards, Round 3 has 3 cards, and so on, up to a maximum hand size (usually 7 to
10 cards, depending on player count).
- Going Down: After the peak round, the hand size decreases by 1 card each round
until you finish the final round with 1 card.
- Step-by-Step Gameplay
   * [Deal Cards] ➔ [Flip Trump] ➔ [Players Bid] ➔ [Play Tricks] ➔ [Score Round]

### Deal Cards:
The dealer distributes the correct number of cards for that round to each
player, one at a time.

### Flip (Determine) Trump:
he dealer places the remaining deck in the center and flips the top card face
up. The suit of this card is the trump suit for the round. (If cards run out on
the maximum round, play the round with "No Trump").

### Bidding:
Starting to the dealer’s left, each player evaluates their hand and bids the
exact number of tricks they think they will win. A scorekeeper records these
bids.

### Playing Tricks:
The player to the dealer's left plays the first card.

### The Hook Rule (Optional but Recommended):
To ensure not everyone makes their bid, the dealer cannot make a bid that makes
the total number of bids equal the number of available tricks. Someone must
fail.

## Rules For Scoring
- Players must follow suit if they can.
- If a player cannot follow suit, they can play a trump card to try to win, or
discard an unwanted card from a different suit.
- The trick is won by the highest card of the led suit, or by the highest trump
card played.
- The winner of the trick leads the next one.

## Scoring System
- Scoring rewards precision, not just winning tricks.
- Making Your Bid:
    * If you win the exact number of tricks you bid, you get 10 points plus 1
    point per trick won (e.g., bidding 3 and winning 3 gets you 13
    points). Bidding 0 and winning 0 gets you exactly 10 points.
- Missing Your Bid:
    * If you win more or fewer tricks than your bid, you get 0 points for that
    round.
    * The player with the highest total score at the end of the entire "Up and
    Down" cycle wins the game.


## Additional Notes
### Winning Strategies
#### Bidding Zero
Low cards like 2s, 3s, and 4s are highly valuable for safely bidding zero. Lead
them early to shed them before other players run out of a suit.

#### The Power of the Lead
Winning a trick gives you the lead, which forces you to control the next
suit. If you have already hit your bid, avoid winning tricks at all costs by
playing your lowest possible valid cards.

#### Watch the Trump Count
Keep track of how many trump cards have been played during a round. An average
trump card can become the highest remaining card if the top trumps have already
been cleared.

### Equation for PEAK cards to deal

[51/P] = PEAK
